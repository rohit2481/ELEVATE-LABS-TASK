********************************** Common Password Attacks ***************************************

Common password attacks are techniques used to obtain or guess a user's password. Below are the most common types:

1. Brute-Force Attack

* Tries every possible password combination until the correct one is found.
* More effective against short or simple passwords.

2. Dictionary Attack

* Uses a list of common words, phrases, and leaked passwords instead of trying every combination.
* Effective against passwords like "password123" or "welcome".

3. Credential Stuffing

* Uses usernames and passwords leaked from one website to try logging into other websites.
* Works because many people reuse passwords across multiple accounts.

4. Password Spraying

* Tries a few common passwords (such as "Summer2025!" or "Welcome123") against many different user accounts.
* Helps attackers avoid account lockouts.

5. Phishing

* Tricks users into entering their passwords on fake websites or through fraudulent emails, messages, or calls.
* Relies on deception rather than technical hacking.

6. Keylogging

* Malware records everything typed on a keyboard, including passwords.
* The captured information is sent to the attacker.

7. Shoulder Surfing

* An attacker watches someone type their password in public or records it with a camera.

8. Rainbow Table Attack

* Uses precomputed tables of hashed passwords to reverse weak password hashes.
* Modern security practices such as unique salts make this attack much less effective.

9. Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) Attack

* Intercepts communication between a user and a website, potentially capturing login credentials if the connection is not properly secured.

10. Social Engineering

* Manipulates people into revealing passwords by pretending to be a trusted person or organization.

********************* How to Protect Yourself **********************

* Use long, unique passwords for every account.
* Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) whenever possible.
* Use a password manager to generate and store strong passwords.
* Avoid clicking suspicious links or downloading unknown attachments.
* Keep your devices and software updated.
* Never reuse passwords across different accounts.

Using strong, unique passwords together with MFA is one of the most effective ways to reduce the risk from many of these attacks.
